# Motivation ### Progression after Certification * Lots of resources — little guidance * Most information is about paragliding in general * Site guides written by and for experienced pilots
* Today I want to talk about progression * Specifically during the period after pilots obtain P3 certification * Expand on an idea for getting the community to fill in the gaps As a new P3... * There are a lot of resources out there * But there's no longer an instructor guiding you at every step * Lacking the structure of certification guidelines However * Everyone learns at a different pace * Some people are good at muddling through on their own * Others need more structure * So, are you on your own? # Paragliding Community * Help newer pilots develop necessary skills * Ensure safety and fun for all participants
* The paragliding community is here for you * Feel free to ask around for advice * Nobody is going to think less of you * Everyone is willing to share things they've picked up through the years
# Existing Resources * Books & Videos * Sprints & Clinics * Mentors
* Books & Videos * A lot of good information * General knowledge * Not tailored to site or even a region * Not obvious for newer pilots how to incorporate these insights * Clinics and Sprints * Lots of fun * Good for breaking through roadblocks * Long gap between attending them * Expensive * Mentorship * Best if available * Fill in gaps left by instructors * Does not scale well * Effort intensive * Depends on the physical availability of volunteers
# Proposal * Pilot program * Collection of small tasks * Tailored for sites in SoCal * Focus on building specific skills
* To address shortcomings, a little experiment * Small tasks alone relatively pointless * Task is merely a context * Familiarity with site beyond site guides * Improving specific skills * Experimentation is the key to growth * Tasks inspire to try new things or doing things differently
